# 4. Critical Thinking & Bias Awareness Cheat Sheet

### 1. Common Analytical Paradoxes
Be careful when aggregating or interpreting data.
*   **Simpson’s Paradox**: A trend appears in several groups but disappears or reverses when groups are combined. (Always check sub-segments!).
*   **Base Rate Fallacy**: Ignoring the "general probability" (the base rate) when looking at specific cases.
*   **Berkson's Paradox**: A false correlation created by a biased sample (e.g., only looking at successful startups).

### 2. Sample Size & Significance
Avoid drawing conclusions too early.
*   **Law of Large Numbers**: As a sample size grows, its mean gets closer to the population average.
*   **Law of Small Numbers**: Small samples are prone to extreme variation; don't mistake "noise" for a trend.
*   **Survivorship Bias**: Drawing conclusions from a group that "survived" a selection process while ignoring those that didn't.

### 3. Correlation vs. Causation
*   **Spurious Correlation**: Two variables moving together by chance (e.g., Ice cream sales and forest fires).
*   **Confounding Variable**: An outside factor that affects both variables (e.g., High temperature).
