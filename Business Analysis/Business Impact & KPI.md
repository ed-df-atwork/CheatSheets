# Business Impact & KPI Frameworks Cheat Sheet

### 1. The North Star Framework
The single most important metric that measures the core value your product delivers to customers.
*   **North Star**: Monthly Active Users (Engagement) or Total GMV (Revenue).
*   **Input Metrics**: The smaller, actionable behaviors that drive the North Star (e.g., "Add to Cart" rate).

### 2. Standard Business KPIs
Common formulas for measuring health and efficiency.
*   **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)**: Total S&M Spend / New Customers Acquired.
*   **Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)**: (Avg Order Value × Purchase Frequency) × Customer Lifespan.
*   **Churn Rate**: (Customers lost during period / Total customers at start of period) × 100.
*   **ARPU (Avg Revenue Per User)**: Total Revenue / Total Active Users.

### 3. Calculating Growth & Variation
Standardize how you report changes over time.

-- Month-over-Month (MoM) Growth
Growth_Rate = ((Current_Month - Previous_Month) / Previous_Month) * 100

-- Variance to Target
Variance = ((Actual_Result - Target_Goal) / Target_Goal) * 100

### 4. Cohort Analysis Basics
Grouping users by a shared starting event to track behavior over time.
*   **Retention Cohort**: Tracking users who signed up in 'January' vs 'February' to see who stays active longer.
*   **Action**: Use this to determine if product updates improved long-term stickiness.

### 5. Statistical vs. Business Significance
The "So What?" test for data analysts.
*   **Statistical Significance**: Is the result likely due to chance? (p-value < 0.05).
*   **Business Significance**: Is the result large enough to matter? (e.g., a $1k lift may be significant but not worth a $100k project).
*   **Effect Size**: Always report the actual impact (e.g., "Projected $200k annual lift").

### 6. The "A-A-R-R-R" Pirate Metrics
A framework for mapping the customer journey.
*   **Acquisition**: Where are users coming from?
*   **Activation**: Do they have a great first experience?
*   **Retention**: Do they come back?
*   **Referral**: Do they tell others?
*   **Revenue**: How do you make money?
